First Look at the RTX 5090

Date: June 2, 2025

NVIDIA has officially unveiled the RTX 5090 — the flagship next-generation graphics card. With even more power, an improved architecture, and revolutionary energy efficiency, the RTX 5090 promises to be a leader in graphics technology.

Key Specifications of the RTX 5090:

  • New Blackwell architecture
  • 32 GB GDDR7 video memory
  • 3rd generation ray tracing support
  • DLSS 4.0 with neural network upscaling
  • Power consumption: 450W
  • PCIe 5.0, HDMI 2.1a, DisplayPort 2.1

RTX 5090 in Games

The image showcases the impressive gaming capabilities of the RTX 5090. Thanks to next-generation power, users can enjoy games at maximum settings in 4K resolution and beyond, without compromising quality or frame rates.

Benchmark Results:

  • Black Myth: Wukong (4K, High, RT): 85 FPS
  • Cyberpunk 2077 (4K, Ultra, RT): 95 FPS
  • Hogwarts Legacy (4K, Ultra): 145 FPS
  • Forza Horizon 5 (4K, Extreme): 190 FPS
  • Red Dead Redemption 2 (4K, Ultra): 70 FPS

Analysis and Conclusions:

The RTX 5090 shows a significant performance boost over the RTX 4090, especially in games with active ray tracing and high resolutions. It also maintains excellent power-to-stability ratio.

This graphics card will be an essential tool for enthusiasts, streamers, game developers, and professionals working with 3D graphics and neural networks.

News and Rumors:

According to the latest reports, NVIDIA plans to release a special limited edition RTX 5090 Titan with even higher TDP and an improved cooling system. There's also discussion about the integration of new AI features directly at the driver level.

Additionally, partner models from ASUS, MSI, and Gigabyte are expected, featuring custom designs, RGB lighting, and water cooling.
